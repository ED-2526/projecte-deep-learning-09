SELECTING A PROJECT
What is a good topic, proposed topics
6
What is a good topic?
(why we selected the ones we did)
• Can it be solved with the architectures and techniques we have seen during the 
course?
– CNN, RNN, Contrastive Learning (, Attention)
• Is it the right complexity?
– We want to do end-to-end training (avoid using tabular data of pre-extracted features)
– Requires more than sklearn and classical ML methods
– It is not too complex (we only have 5 weeks)
• Is it the right size, model wise?
– The kind of model you need should be moderate size: fits in GPU memory, reasonable number 
of trainable parameters (you can train it in a few hours)
– Don’t try to train a huge model from scratch, build on the shoulders of giants and finetune
– Even pre-trained models might be too big for your GPU memory
– Should be small enough (modular enough) to be understandable
• Is it the right size, data wise?
– You need to have enough (annotated) data for training. If data is not already available, it’s not 
a good project (you have no time to prepare data from scratch)
– You have no resources to train with the whole internet, not even with the whole MS-Coco 
dataset…
– Your data should fit in local disk space (for fast access during training): max 10-20 GB
